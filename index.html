Things to Do in Seattle

See our full Seattle recommendations map →
Attractions
Space Needle
400 Broad Street
Seattle's iconic landmark. The top rotates slowly for a full 360° view! MoPOP and Chihuly Garden and Glasshouse are right next door.
Pike Place Market
Downtown Seattle
A must-visit! Stalls start packing up around 5 PM, even though it's listed open till 6.
Waterfront Park
1401 Alaskan Way
A newly rebuilt waterfront walkway with sweeping views of Puget Sound and the Olympic Mountains.
MOHAI (Museum of History & Industry)
860 Terry Avenue North
Learn the history of Seattle and Puget Sound, with a nice walk along Lake Union.
Coffee & Cafes
Uptown Espresso (Belltown)
2504 4th Avenue
Varsha & Mat's favorite — some of the smoothest coffee in the city.
Top Pot Doughnuts
2124 5th Avenue
A Seattle classic — try the maple bar. Even Obama stopped by once!
Macrina Bakery & Cafe
1943 1st Avenue South
Fresh-baked bread and seasonal treats. The cornettos are lighter than they look!
Citizen Collective
706 Taylor Avenue North
Part beer garden, part coffee shop, with great brunch food next to the Space Needle.
Drinks
Cottontail
2219 4th Avenue — also our after party venue!
A hidden speakeasy-style bar — look for the large mural outside the entrance.
Cloudburst Brewing
2116 Western Avenue
A local craft brewery specializing in IPAs — small but great atmosphere.
Zig Zag Cafe
1501 Western Avenue
Credited with reviving the Last Word cocktail. Also serves food and house-made ice cream.
Food
Big Mario's Pizza
815 5th Avenue North
NYC-worthy pizza by the slice — worth the walk up Queen Anne hill.
Biscuit Bitch (Belltown)
2303 3rd Avenue
Loaded biscuit breakfasts. Order ahead online to skip the line.
FOB Poke Bar
220 Blanchard Street
Build-your-own poke bowls. Sister shop FOB Sushi Bar is a block away.

Good to Know

After Party ID
Cottontail is 21+ only — bring a valid government-issued ID for entry.
Unplugged Ceremony
We're keeping the ceremony unplugged — please silence and put away phones and cameras during the vows so everyone can be fully present. Snap away at the reception and after!
Weather & What to Bring
Late-summer Seattle is usually warm and dry during the day, cooling off in the evenings. Sunscreen and sunglasses for the outdoor ceremony, and a light layer for after dark, are a good idea.
Cards and Gifts
There will be a designated box for cards at the reception if you'd like to drop one off. Our registry is linked on the wedding website if you're looking for gift ideas.
Space for Babies & Nursing Mothers
There will be a quiet, dedicated space available for babies and nursing mothers at both the Friday Welcome Event and the Saturday Ceremony & Reception.
Sunday Checkout & Luggage
Standard hotel checkout is typically 11 AM. If you're heading to brunch before a later flight, ask the front desk about holding your luggage so you don't have to lug it to Golden Gardens.
